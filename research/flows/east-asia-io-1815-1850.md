## 2. Indian Ocean W — per-decade guidance

- **India↔Britain**: step-up from 1815 (Charter Act 1813; 20+ private
  vessels by 1814; cotton goods ~0.8M yards 1814 → ~51M by 1835), growth
  every decade, second step after 1833 on the eastward legs; Calcutta
  leads, Bombay accelerates 1830s–40s. (Counted tonnage series exists
  unfetched — class proxied, direction high.)
- **NEW zanzibar** (reconstructed/proxied): dormant 1810s → rising 1820s
  (the Shivji customs-farm rent, MT$110,000 from 1819 → MT$220,000 by
  1856, is the growth proxy — verified against Sheriff) → strong 1830s
  (US treaty signed Muscat 21 Sep 1833; Salem consul 1837; the
  "32 American ships in two years" anchor is unpinnable — downgrade or
  pin, P-09 ⚠; clove boom 1835–45) → 1840s peak-and-wobble (clove price
  collapse; ivory/copal climb). Lanes: ↔Muscat (monsoon; Said's marine
  ~10–20 square-riggers + dhow fleets), ↔Bombay/Kutch, ↔Salem/US, Mrima
  feeders. The East African slave trade into the coast continues in this
  period — if surfaced, full sober pattern.
- **NEW mauritius-sugar** (counted colonial series — shape verified, the
  seven 5-year figures must be pinned to their table before parameters,
  P-08 ⚠): ~11k t/yr (early 1820s) → ~20k (late 1820s, after the 1825
  duty equalization) → ~33k (1830s; flat 1835–39 at abolition) → ~35k →
  ~56k (late 1840s). Port Louis → London/Liverpool; rice in from India;
  Madagascar feeders.
- **NEW indenture** (counted; framing text §4): Calcutta + Madras (from
  1843, substantial c.1850) → Port Louis: 1834 first ship (Atlas, 36
  people) → ~25,468 by May 1839 → **suspension 29 May 1839–2 Dec 1842
  over documented abuses** → 1843 spike (34,525; 30,218 m / 4,307 f) →
  ~11,549 (1844) → late-1840s trough → cumulative [75k–95k] by 1850.
- **mascarene coerced tail**: the French-era lanes (E5 verified — Allen's
  641 voyages exact; totals 160k floor / 243k Allen ceiling for 1670–1810;
  whole-trade floor corrected to 336k) go ILLEGAL 1811–c.1827:
  **~52,550 people to Mauritius alone (Allen JICH 2008 — the published
  point the gather missed; band [30k,60k] Mauritius+Seychelles,
  P-04 ⚠ inverted)**, then stop; the system re-centers on sugar out +
  labor ships in.
- **Red Sea**: Mocha fades decade over decade (world-market force finished
  by the 1820s; Egyptian occupation 1833–c.1840); Jeddah level throughout;
  steam on Bombay–Suez from **March 1830** (the Hugh Lindsay's first
  voyage — date corrected, P-16 ⚠) stays behind the boundary.
- **Gulf**: flat Basra/Bushehr; the Qawasim hazard switches OFF after the
  General Maritime Treaty **signed 8 Jan 1820** (corrected; fully off
  after 1835); pearling as seasonal grounds-traffic, [1,500–4,000] boats
  (Wellsted's high count discounted).

## 4. Framing texts staged for user review (X-R2)

**indenture.framing:** "These vessels carried indentured laborers from
India to Mauritius under contract, into a plantation labor regime built
directly on the site of slavery. Recruitment abuses were documented from
the start — emigration was suspended from 1839 to 1842 for that reason —
and mortality on the passage was real. The system is shown factually:
people under contract, not cargo."

**amoy-emigration.framing (nanyang component, from 1847):** "From 1847
vessels carried indentured emigrants from Amoy toward Cuba and Peru under
contracts many did not understand; mortality on these passages was high.
The traffic is shown factually and is never a reward."
