## 1. Baltic/North Sea — per-decade guidance

The 1815–50 Baltic story is **re-weighting, not new geography**: the Sound
passage index doubles (~8,500→~20,000, ≈2.3%/yr, R-01/R-32 ✅ verified —
the British-wheat backbone R-11/R-12 confirmed to the digit against Sharp
2010's appendix, 1,144 qr in 1833 and 87,701 in 1845 exact), with a deep
mid-1830s trough and a post-1846 step-change. Only one genuinely new system
(German emigration) and it folds to the Atlantic.

- **baltic-grain-west** (re-weight, counted): 1810s hold with lane weight
  to **Amsterdam/Hamburg** (the Corn Laws shut London); 1820s mild
  recovery; 1830s SPLIT — the deep UK trough 1832–37 is a **floor, not a
  zero** (grain moved into bond and to the continent; R-11 ✅), then the
  1838–42 surge; 1840s strong, **POST-1846 STEP-CHANGE 1.5–2×** with the UK
  lanes taking the growth (R-12 ✅). Danzig stays Europe's #2 wheat port
  behind Odessa throughout (R-14 ✅). Flag drift: British largest all era,
  1825 boom peak / 1835 Corn-Law trough (R-02 ✅); Prussian #2 from the
  mid-1820s (R-03 ✅).
- **baltic-timber-naval-west** (thin the London lanes, counted/reconstructed):
  **halve the London-lane shares 1815–40** — ~80% of British softwood is
  now North American behind the ~275% preferential wall (R-16 ✅; the big
  equalization was 1851, outside the window) — while the **continental
  lanes hold undistorted**: Baltic timber to Britain is thinner but never
  absent (607,533 loads to Britain in 1840; 1,000+ Baltic timber ships for
  England through the Sound in 1815 vs 172 in 1785, R-17 ✅). Stepwise
  recovery after the 1842/1846 reductions; **nudge Memel up** (fir ≈2×
  Riga's, R-18 ✅).
- **swedish-iron-west** (step down, reconstructed): the arc is confirmed —
  decline under puddling, the US becoming the largest buyer, the British
  niche narrowing to **oregrounds for cementation steel** (R-19 ✅ on
  direction). **⚠ CORRECTION (R-19):** the ">52,000 t/yr to Britain in the
  1790s" headline is inflated — it conflates total exports / all
  destinations; Britain's intake at the 18th-c peak was nearer **~20–30,000
  t**, already past its peak by the 1790s. Step down ~15%/decade 1815–45,
  flat after; the displaced volume went to the US (out of basin — silences
  cross-ref).
- **petersburg-west** (strong growth, proxied/reconstructed): grows every
  decade, **mostly foreign-carried** (chiefly British bottoms — the
  canonical fact of the trade, R-06 ✅); hemp ~2M poods (~33,000 t) + flax
  ~0.6M poods (~10,000 t) + **tallow the top value item** ~3.81M poods
  (~62,000 t) ≈ £2.2M to mid-1840s (R-20/R-21 ✅ — all three pood→tonne
  conversions arithmetically clean; the author's flagged arithmetic fix is
  sound). London share ≥0.55; ~1,200–1,500 arrivals/yr (1820s) → 2,000+
  (1840s) with the 1830s dip (R-22 proxied — no per-decade series exists;
  interpolate on the Sound shape, declared method ✅). Plate/Australian
  tallow competition arrives only in the 1850s (epilogue).
- **norway-timber-fish-west** (+~30%/decade, reconstructed): the
  fastest-growing major Sound flag (R-04 ✅); 82% of Norwegian timber to
  UK/France/Low Countries (1826–30), a retained UK duty edge over the
  Baltic (R-29 ✅); tilt toward London. **Biggest jump lands post-1849 in
  the epilogue decade** (Navigation Acts repeal — R-28 ✅ on direction; the
  exact "+191% 1850–60" and "Dutch/Danish passages ~double 1840→1851–56"
  percentages are FOLLOW-UP, unpinned).
- **english-coal-foreign** (growing, proxied): coal among the principal
  Baltic imports by mid-century, with salt + colonial goods on the
  return-east legs (R-30 ✅); ADD small Copenhagen + St Petersburg
  destination lanes by the 1830s–40s. The great coal-export age is
  post-1850.
- **north-sea-shorthaul** (sail holds; steam boundary): from the GSN
  (inc. 1824) the **packet layer** — passengers/mails/cattle/fine goods on
  regular London–Hamburg/Rotterdam/Ostend/Boulogne runs, ~40 steamers by
  1840 — passes behind the declared steam boundary (R-26 ✅); bulk stays
  sail. Evidence note per decade rather than shrinkage before 1845; late
  1840s shave 10–15% + boundary marker.
- **NEW german-emigration-atlantic** (from ~1830, folds to the ATLANTIC
  basin): Bremen/Bremerhaven (outport 1827) → USA, ~10,000 emigrants/yr by
  1832; tobacco/cotton/flour return (R-25 ✅). **HONEST NOTE preserved:** in
  the 1840s more Germans still left via Le Havre/Rotterdam/Antwerp/
  Liverpool — German-port dominance is POST-1850. Belongs to the Atlantic
  fold, not a new Baltic system.

**Corn-Law attribution (R-31 asserted, kept — NOT upgraded):** the −32.6%
