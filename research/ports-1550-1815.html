4Method, units & caveats

Three lenses, three answers. Ship movements favour dense coasting and bulk hubs; tonnage rewards large ships (East Indiamen, colliers, Baltic bulk); value rewards rich cargoes (silver, spices, tea, sugar). The famous long-distance ports — Batavia, Canton, Havana — barely register by hull count yet climb high by tonnage and value.

Units are not interchangeable. Sound Toll data count passages; Dutch-Asiatic Shipping counts VOC voyages; the Carrera is in ships and tons; English series are customs clearances; value is a reasoned reconstruction from commodity mixes, not a ledger. The cross-regional ordering is synthesis, not arithmetic.

Where the record is thin. Sound Toll destination ports appear only from the mid-1660s; pre-1618 cargo data carry 25–50% fraud; Danish (throughout) and Swedish (1650–1710) ships were toll-exempt. So the earliest decades lean on port-city and trade-route scholarship. The 1810s covers only 1810–1815.
