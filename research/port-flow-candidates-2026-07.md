## Tier 1 — counted series, large flows, high charter value

### 1. The Río de la Plata complex (Montevideo + Buenos Aires) — Atlantic
- **Era:** 1680–1815 (ramping hard from ~1760, not 1778 — the acceleration
  precedes the Reglamento de Libre Comercio).
- **Magnitude (counted):** Jumar's ship-entry reconstruction 1680–1806
  (departure licences + registros; 1779–1806 from the Montevideo
  governor-viceroy correspondence, AGN Sala IX). Order-of-magnitude growth:
  fragatas 22 (1680–1760) → 107 (1760–80) → 308 (1780–1800); bergantines
  0 → 4 → 207. Post-1780 destinations: Cádiz 36 %, north-Spanish ports 27 %,
  Mediterranean Spain 37 %.
- **Coerced flow (counted, sober framing):** Borucki's voyage-level database —
  **712 slave voyages 1777–1812, ≥ 70,000 enslaved people disembarked**
  (≥ 77,500 embarked); 60 % of people and 77.5 % of voyages via BRAZIL
  (Rio alone ≥ 333 voyages), the rest direct from Africa — Mozambique Island
  the principal African source (≥ 12,600 embarked; + 3,400 Quelimane/Kilwa).
  The Crown made **Montevideo the sole authorized slave-entry port in 1791**.
  A 1790s triangle carried tasajo (salt beef) to Cuba to feed the enslaved
  plantation population. Middle-Passage invariant applies to every leg.
- **Also:** pre-1760 Buenos Aires contraband silver (Seville deliberately
  choked the legal Plata outlet) — an *evasion*-class flow; candidate
  silences entry if the node bakes before the flow is authored.
- **Sim shape:** one node ("Montevideo (Río de la Plata)") serving the
  complex; lanes to Cádiz, Barcelona (Med proxy), Rio, Bahia, Havana (tasajo),
  Mozambique-side source lane. Joins the Atlantic basin.
- **Evidence class:** counted (Jumar; Borucki/TSTD).
  Sources: ri.conicet.gov.ar (Jumar school); Borucki 2011.

### 2. Basra + the Gulf run (with Bandar Abbas ← queued t2, upgraded) — Indian Ocean W
- **Era:** 1550–1815 (Basra continuous; Bandar Abbas factory era 1623–1763).
- **Verified ✅ (3–0):** the EIC **Gombroon diaries** survive as a continuous
  series (IOR/G/29/2-14, thirty-two diaries, Nov 1708–Feb 1763, lacunae
  declared) recording **named ship movements** across Bandar Abbas, Bombay,
  Basra, Bandar-e Rig, Surat, Bandar-e Charak, Mocha, Muscat, Bushehr; the
  factory dates from 1623, straight after Hormuz fell ✅; the 1786 Ottoman
  presence at Basra was 11 large + 2 small ships ✅; and the **Çelebi family
  of Surat ran fifteen ships across the Indian Ocean 1759–80** per EIC/VOC
  registers ✅ — non-European, Ottoman-subject private shipping, exactly the
  agency the charter wants charted.
- **Magnitude (counted, unverified):** Leiden dissertation on VOC Basra:
  ~11 large-vessel arrivals/yr 1724–56; FY 1727-28 = 15 foreign arrivals
  (3 Surat Muslim ships, 2 from Mocha); boom > 25/yr 1766–74; collapse after
  the 1773 plague + Zand occupation (customs from 3–4,000 tumans → near
  nothing). Bushehr: 27 arrivals Aug 1737–Apr 1738, mostly local tranki.
- **Sim shape:** add **Basra**; promote **Bandar Abbas** (t2 → next); lanes to
  Surat, Muscat, Mocha, Bombay — the anchors already exist. Extends the
  existing gulf systems; the Gombroon diaries make it counted/proxied, and
  the pre-1622 leg is the **Hormuz** predecessor (era-name candidate:
  "Hormuz" → "Bandar Abbas" at 1622, the pattern Louisbourg set).
- **Evidence class:** counted/proxied (IOR/G/29; VOC Basra lists).

### 3. British Southern Whale Fishery + American offshore whaling — Atlantic/Pacific grounds
- **Era:** 1775–1815 (BSWF); American colonial whaling from ~1715 (Nantucket).
- **Magnitude (counted):** the BSWF database (Jones/Chatwin/Richards,
  whalinghistory.org) — **2,500+ voyages, 900+ vessels, 1775–1859** (~30
  departures/yr average), each with port, dates, rig, tonnage, master,
  grounds, cargo returns. The AOWV database covers American voyages from the
  1700s. Scottish Arctic whaling has its own database (Sanger).
- **Sim shape:** a **grounds node** (the Smeerenburg pattern — a station dot
  standing for a fishery): "the South Sea grounds" off Peru/Brazil banks,
  lanes from London (and Boston/Nantucket for AOWV). **Revises the
  svalbard-offshore-whaling silences entry**: the same grounds-node solution
  retro-fits the Greenland/Davis Strait fishery (Dutch series 1661–1800 are
  famously counted; Scottish SAW likewise) — the register's "no node by its
  nature" reasoning weakens once a grounds node exists anywhere.
- **Evidence class:** counted (voyage-level databases, public).

### 4. Hudson's Bay Company: London ↔ Hudson Bay — Atlantic/Arctic
- **Era:** 1668–1815, continuous.
- **Magnitude (counted):** HBCA ships' histories (Archives of Manitoba) — a
  per-vessel register (name, rig, HBC service dates, service areas) from the
  1668 Eaglet/Nonsuch onward, through the era's end (Nimble 1792–93, Beaver
  1788–93). Real volume is small — 1–3 supply ships/yr — the **Dejima
  pattern: a low-volume, high-meaning exemplar**, and the only English
  sub-Arctic presence on the chart.
- **Sim shape:** one node ("York Factory (Hudson Bay)"), one London lane,
  jja/son season-gated like Arkhangelsk (Hudson Strait ice) — the seasonal
  Arctic corridor machinery already exists.
- **Evidence class:** counted (HBCA).

### 5. The Mascarene slave trade (Port Louis, Île de France) — Indian Ocean W
- **Era:** 1721–1815 (French Mascarenes; peak 1768–1809).
- **Magnitude (counted, coerced — sober framing mandatory):** a **641-voyage
  inventory, Mauritius/Réunion 1768–1809** (Allen, JAH 2008); catchment
  Madagascar, Mozambique, the Swahili coast, India, Southeast Asia, West
  Africa — the flow that binds the western Indian Ocean to the Atlantic
  pattern of coerced movement.
- **Sim shape:** Port Louis node; lanes from Mozambique-side and Madagascar
  (Île Sainte-Marie is already queued t3 — this is its flow); extends the
  registered **coerced-flows-beyond-atlantic** silence from asserted toward
  counted — **a direct silences-register answer**.
- **Evidence class:** counted (Allen's inventory), quantified framing
  validator-enforced per charter rule 6.

## Tier 2 — counted/proxied, medium volume or medium fit

### 6. Jeddah — Red Sea (new water for the chart)
Ottoman tithe on Indian shipping documented (1698 allocation of 5,000 kuruş
from the Indian-vessel tithe to the emir of Mecca); the coffee staple's
northbound leg (Mocha → Jeddah → Suez/Istanbul) and the pilgrimage traffic.
Era 1550–1815. Evidence: proxied (customs/tithe records; Panzac; Faroqhi).
Joins Mocha (exists) to Istanbul/Alexandria. A 1785 record puts Istanbul's
imports from India (10 M livres) near parity with imports from Europe
(12 M) — the India–Ottoman axis is a genuinely missing cross-basin artery.

### 7. The Ottoman caravane maritime + Alexandria & Salonica — Mediterranean
Panzac's flow: French (later Greek) hulls carrying Ottoman merchants' cargo
between Ottoman ports, 1680–1830 — Istanbul, Smyrna (queued t2 — upgrade),
Alexandria, Salonica, Chios, Acre. One counted episode: 1772, **60+ French
and 9 English ships** lifted Macedonian wheat Salonica → Istanbul during the
Russian blockade. Evidence: proxied/reconstructed (Panzac's series). Adds
the empire's internal seaborne metabolism the rankings never saw; pairs with
the existing ottoman-provisioning system.

### 8. Ragusa (Dubrovnik) — Mediterranean (queued t2 — upgraded)
Mid-16th-century Ragusan fleets ran regular lanes to Bari, Messina, Naples,
Genoa, Livorno, Barcelona, Málaga — and the **Dubrovnik State Archives'
notarial/chancery/insurance series (Tadić)** make it counted/proxied, not
asserted. The queue already holds it; this research upgrades its evidence
class and priority. ~180 ships at peak; declines after 1600.

### 9. Callao (Lima) — the Pacific coastal thalassic — Pacific
Callao hubbed an intra-American Pacific trade reaching Acapulco north and
Concepción south (Armada del Mar del Sur; almojarifazgo records) — the sim's
Pacific currently knows only the galleon and Sitka. Era 1550–1815; evidence
proxied/reconstructed. Would give Acapulco a second, non-galleon connection
and chart Spanish-American coastal agency. (Bake note: needs the Humboldt
current polars — the archived fields cover it; Panama seal stays.)

### 10. Mozambique Island — Indian Ocean W / Atlantic hinge
Already implied by #1 and #5: ≥ 12,600 enslaved people embarked for the Río
de la Plata alone (Borucki), plus the Portuguese carreira way-station role
and the Mascarene supply. Counted on the Plata side, proxied elsewhere.
Sober framing. Arguably supersedes Mombasa (t3) as the East-African node.
