The chart’s silences

The archive is not the past. Every dataset behind this chart began as someone’s decision to count — a toll register, a customs house, a company ledger — and the seas those institutions did not watch left no numbers behind. This page records the flows we know existed and cannot (or choose not to) quantify: each with the reason for its silence, and what the chart does about it.

Every trade in the simulation carries an evidence class — counted (a surviving series), proxied (inferred from adjacent records), reconstructed (scholarship’s estimate), or asserted (our own stated guess, wide bounds, reasoning on record). The rule beneath them all: a known trade is never silently zero — because in a living chart, zero is not neutrality; it is a claim, repeated every simulated day, that the ships weren’t there.

english coastal colliers silence

answered — assertedexcluded by a declared basisBritain (Tyne & Wear → London)

The ships rankings count foreign-going clearances (R1 decision), so Europe's single largest generator of ship movements is invisible to them. Answered: the flow matrix carries a reconstructed collier system (~150 voyages/yr in the 1550s → ~4,000–6,500/yr by 1810).

Answered by the flow system english-coastal-colliers — its voyages sail the chart, and their ledgers say so.

coastal shipping general

gesturednever recordedevery coast, all basins

Coasting dwarfed foreign-going traffic in vessel numbers everywhere — grain hoys, wine barks, fish carriers, timber floats. No source counts it; no honest range exists at world scale. Acknowledged as the sea's ambient metabolism; candidate for ambient rendering (S3) rather than a volume claim.

north sea herring fishery

gestureda fishery, not a laneDutch Republic (the grote visserij)

Hundreds of herring busses at sea every season — among the largest fleets afloat, but grounds-traffic rather than port-to-port trade, so it fits no lane. Candidate ambient feature; the fishery's SIZE is well documented even though it has no origin→destination shape.

sound toll evasion

excluded, on recordsilent by evasionBaltic (the counted stratum itself)

The anchoring series is itself distorted: under-declaration, wartime neutral re-flagging, and the Swedish exemption (1645–1710) all bias the registers the 'counted' systems rest on. Carried as a caveat on evidence=counted rather than as a flow — the register records that even the archive's firmest ground is soft.

small craft inshore

excluded, on recordnever recordedall basins

Boats below burden thresholds — wherries, prams, coasters under ~20 tons — were never registered anywhere. Below the simulation's vessel scale; excluded knowingly.

caribbean smuggling silence

answered — assertedsilent by evasionCaribbean (the free ports and the asiento's shadow)

Silent BY EVASION — unrecorded because recording was the danger. Answered at R3: the atlantic basin carries an asserted system (±60% bounds around free-port scholarship's central guesses; St Eustatius alone logged 3,000+ ship calls at its 1770s peak, most of them the traffic everyone else's customs never saw).

Answered by the flow system caribbean-smuggling — its voyages sail the chart, and their ledgers say so.

junk trade under registration

answered — assertednever recordedEast & Southeast Asia

The great structural silence of the European record: the Nanyang junk trade generated no western toll series, yet the Nagasaki registers — where a state DID count — show Chinese hulls outnumbering Dutch ten to one. Answered at R3 by reconstruction (Blussé, Reid, Sugihara), not assertion: the scholarship is strong enough to class it reconstructed.

Answered by the flow system nanyang-junk-trade — its voyages sail the chart, and their ledgers say so.

china coastal silence

answered — assertedexcluded by a declared basisthe Chinese littoral

The coastal junk metabolism of the world's largest economy — the Shanghai shachuan grain fleet alone counted in the thousands. Answered at R3 (asserted, ±60%) on the same consistency grounds as the English colliers: if Newcastle's coasting is in, Tianjin's is.

Answered by the flow system china-coastal-grain — its voyages sail the chart, and their ledgers say so.

african coastwise

gesturednever recordedWest & East African coasts

Canoe-house, lagoon, and coastal commerce along the Gold Coast, the Bight, and the Swahili littoral — real, substantial, organized (the Efik houses ran it on their own terms), and with no defensible voyage unit at the sim's vessel scale. GESTURED (R3 decision): acknowledged here, candidate for ambient representation, no volume claim fabricated.

pacific voyaging

gesturednever recordedOceania

Polynesian and Micronesian seafaring continued throughout this era, entirely outside every European series — no voyage-count basis exists at all. GESTURED (R3 decision): recorded so the chart's Pacific emptiness reads as the archive's silence, not the ocean's.
