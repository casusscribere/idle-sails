The chart's silences ↗

The flows we know existed and cannot — or choose not to — quantify: coastal metabolisms, fisheries, trades silent by evasion, and the seas outside every European series. Each silence carries its reason and its treatment; three have been answered with stated estimates that now sail the chart.

How the routes are checked ↗

The routing engine is the least-evidenced part of the project: its wind field is a parametric model of about fifteen constants, never tested against the record. This is the beginning of that test — and it leads with how little it can yet say, because a pass rate over the few routes with surviving evidence would launder a narrow check into a broad reassurance.

Route persistence, 1550–1815 ↗

A structural trade-lane model: each top-10 port's principal routes, activated by decade and ranked by staying power — 165 lanes from 1,818 port-decade entries. Filterable by port, region, or cargo.

On the data. These are scholarly best-effort reconstructions, not censuses. The port rankings are anchored to hard regional series (Sound Toll Registers, Dutch-Asiatic Shipping, the Carrera de Indias, English customs) where they exist and are informed estimate elsewhere; the minor-ports roster carries a ◆ where a claim was verified in an adversarial research pass; the route model is explicitly structural.
