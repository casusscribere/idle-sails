Route Persistence
1550 – 1815

The trade lanes that recur most across the age of sail — each port's principal routes, activated in the decades that port ranked among the ten busiest, then ranked by staying power.

⚓ Structural, not empirical. The lanes are each port's genuine principal routes; but there is no historical record of ranked top-ten routes per port per decade. A route counts as "active" in a decade when its origin port sits in that decade's combined top ten (ship-count + tonnage + value) and the decade falls inside the lane's era. Max = 27 decades.
