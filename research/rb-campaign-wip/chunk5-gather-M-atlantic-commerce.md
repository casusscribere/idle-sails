## PATTERNS
DIE: Carrera/Cadiz-America → zero across the 1810s, absent from 1820 (residual Cadiz/Barcelona–Havana 20–60 voyages/yr asserted). British southern whaling: halved mid-1830s, <20 by 1840s. Jamaica sugar HALVES (1820s stable, 1830s −25%, 1840s −50% vs 1810s). Migratory Newfoundland fishery already dead — carrying trade persists flat (200–350 voyages/yr).
CONTINUE: Baltic/Sound dense + growing ~1%/yr (Baltic timber-to-Britain thins — pivot to BNA; grain/iron/naval stores hold). Cuba sugar grows every decade; US whaling grows to the 1846 peak (E3 grounds-node expansion); Plata hides, Brazil sugar/cotton (Bahia/Recife) sustain.
NEW: (1) Cotton: New Orleans(+Mobile/Charleston/Savannah)→Liverpool(+Le Havre ~10–15%): 1820s 150–350 voyages/yr, 1830s 350–700, 1840s 700–1,300 (asserted from counted bales at 800–2,000 bales/ship); NY triangle as ledger texture. (2) BNA timber: Quebec+Saint John/Miramichi→Britain, emigrants westbound: 1,000–1,600 voyages/yr (1820s, anchored 1,520 counted 1819) holding 1,000–1,800 through the 1840s; 1847 famine spike + that year's mortality soberly noted. (3) Emigrant packets: NY↔Liverpool(+London, Le Havre) from 1818: ~25–50 sailings/yr 1820s → 200–400/yr 1840s aggregate (asserted); fixed-cadence spawning. (4) Brazil coffee under British/US carriage: Rio→US (flour out)/Britain/Hamburg: 100–250 voyages/yr 1830s, 200–400 1840s (asserted). (5) South Atlantic republics direct trade: Britain/US/France↔BA/Montevideo (hides/tasajo): tens per flag 1820s → 100–250 all-flags 1840s; blockade dents 1838–40 + 1845–48 (Montevideo picks up calls). (6) Around-the-Horn Pacific: →Valparaíso→Callao: 50–150 voyages/yr 1820s → 150–350 by 1840.
