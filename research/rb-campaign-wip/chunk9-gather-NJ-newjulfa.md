# Chunk 9 gather NJ — New Julfa Armenian carriage (raw, pre-refutation)

**Verdict: a textbook SILENCES-REGISTER case — `gestured` entry. A large real multi-basin flow that moved as carriage on OTHERS' bottoms + overland caravan, no defensible per-lane voyage unit. Do NOT create a standalone Armenian-flag system (would double-count silk already in `greek-ottoman-coasting` + fabricate voyage precision).**

**Identity & dates:** [NJ-01] New Julfa = the Armenian merchant suburb of Isfahan, founded by Shah Abbas I after the deportation from Old Julfa (razed autumn 1604; suburb settled 1605; reconstructed/HIGH). [NJ-02] ~20,000–30,000 deportees; community ~10,000–30,000 across the 17th–18th c (reconstructed/MED). [NJ-03] ~1,000 active long-distance merchants at a time; big firms ran 80–100 factors (reconstructed/asserted MED). [NJ-04] silk-export privilege: Abbas channelled Persia's raw-silk export through the Julfans; **1619 they outbid the EIC at auction** for silk-export control; weakened/lost under Shah Safi (r.1629–42 — one source's "d.1624" is GARBLED, flag; reconstructed HIGH for 1619, MED on the monopoly's legal duration). [NJ-05] **1688 Khoja Phanoos Kalantar agreement with the EIC** (Armenians granted privileges "equal" to English merchants; counted/HIGH — dated treaty). [NJ-06] floruit ~1600–1750; decline: **Afghan/Hotaki siege of Isfahan Mar–Oct 1722** (New Julfa looted, 70,000-toman indemnity, leaders beheaded), then Nader Shah 1746–47 punitive plunder → mass exodus (siege counted/HIGH; indemnity MED).

**Geography of the carriage:** [NJ-07] trans-Eurasian reach (attested destinations): West/Med — Aleppo, Smyrna, Venice, Livorno, Marseille, Amsterdam, London; North — Astrakhan, Moscow, Arkhangelsk, St Petersburg, Sweden (Russia transit); South/East — Surat, Madras/Coromandel, Batavia; Far East — Manila, Canton, Tibet (Lhasa), even Acapulco (via the Manila galleon). Commodities: raw silk above all, + textiles, gems, indigo (reconstructed HIGH for West+IO+Russia; MED for Canton/Tibet/Acapulco = reach not volume). [NJ-08] Tibet/Lhasa rests on the **ledger of Hovhannes Joughayetsi** (pub. Levon Khachikian, *J. Asiatic Soc. of Bengal*, 1966) — a rare quantified single-merchant micro-source (counted at one book's level; Lhasa detail not re-verified, flag).

**Magnitude & class — is any of it counted?** [NJ-09] silk-export mass: ~200,000 kg/yr c.1600 → ~500,000 kg/yr early 18th c (total Persian raw-silk export, Julfan-dominated; reconstructed/MED). [NJ-10] **Russia-transit route partially COUNTED via Russian customs:** ~100,000 kg c.1700; 1676–1685 avg 12,032 kg/yr; 1723–34 >30,000; 1743–47 avg 79,673 kg/yr; mid-18th-c Armenian share 60–70% (counted/proxied MED-HIGH — precision implies real registers). [NJ-11] "good year" ~5,000–6,000 bales @100kg (reconstructed LOW-MED, single-origin). [NJ-12] ~200,000 gold ducats/Levant caravan counter-flow (asserted/reconstructed LOW-MED, single-origin). [NJ-13] **Santa Catharina dossier**: Armenian-freighted ship seized 1748, litigated HCA London 1748–52, preserved ~2,000 pieces of Julfan correspondence (Aslanian's core archive; counted/HIGH). [NJ-14] but these counted bodies quantify the network's INTERNAL life (credit, commenda, correspondence), NOT origin→destination voyages; the carriage appears only in OTHERS' records (EIC/VOC/Portuguese list Armenians as freighters/passengers; Aleppo/Smyrna/Marseille customs booked the silk) — **no series isolates Armenian-account voyages as a countable lane** (reconstructed/HIGH — this absence IS the finding).
