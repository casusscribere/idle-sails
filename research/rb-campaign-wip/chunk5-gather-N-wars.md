# Chunk 5 gather N — wars & naval patterns 1815–1850 (raw, pre-refutation)

[N-01] 100+ privateers fitted out from the US (chiefly Baltimore) under Spanish-American insurgent flags, 3,000+ US sailors (3,500+ under Buenos Aires alone, Bealer). 1815–1821 (tail to 1829). reconstructed/medium — no aggregate prize total retrievable.
[N-02] Five Buenos Aires privateers Jun–Sep 1816 took prizes valued $3M+; singles to $1.5M (Triton, off Cape Verde 31 Dec 1816). 1816–19. reconstructed/medium — contemporary interested-party values; scale not precision.
[N-03] Danels (Irresistible) plundered/sank 26+ Spanish/Portuguese vessels in ~6 weeks; privateers boarded neutrals hunting enemy cargo. 1818–19. counted/high.
[N-04] Buenos Aires suspended letters of marque 1820–21 (US pressure); Artigas commissions lapsed by 1820 — main wave ends; residual tail under Mexican/Colombian colors to ~1829. counted/high.
[N-05] Cochrane blockaded Callao three times (1819, 1819, 1820–21), cut out the Esmeralda 5 Nov 1820; Spanish Pacific trade shut for belligerent flags in windows. counted/high.
[N-06] Last Spanish Pacific frigates surrendered Mar 1822; final Callao blockade ends 23 Jan 1826 — hard end for Pacific-theatre uplift. counted/high.
[N-07] Spanish reconquest convoys few: Morillo 1815 (~60 ships), Barradas Tampico 1829 (capitulated Sept) — the last attempt; after 1829 nil. counted/high.
[N-08] Caribbean piracy (ex-privateers, Cuba/PR): US West Indies Squadron permanent 1822 (~14 warships, 1,500+ men); after Cofresí's capture 1825 piracy "rare". Risk 1818–1825. proxied/high — attack totals (~3,000 quoted) NOT verified, asserted/low if used.
[N-09] Cisplatine War (Dec 1825–Aug 1828): Brazilian blockade of the Plata crippled Argentine commerce; Argentine privateers raided Brazilian shipping; prize totals unverified — keep blockade effect, omit numbers. proxied/medium.
[N-10] Greek privateering licenses from 1821 blurred into piracy as the 1824–25 civil wars collapsed discipline; Aegean piracy peaked 1827, attacking neutrals incl. Smyrna traders. proxied/high — no reliable aggregate count.
[N-11] USS Warren (Oct–Dec 1827): 7 pirate vessels taken/destroyed, 3 merchantmen rescued, 8 American ships CONVOYED Milos→Smyrna (rare post-1815 convoy revival); Doro Passage 16 Oct 1827: ~250 pirates in 5 vessels vs one British brig. counted/high.
[N-12] Navarino (20 Oct 1827) + Grabusa raid (Jan 1828) ended the surge; attacks cease from 1828 (Kapodistrias suppressed island bases). counted/high — Navarino loss figures (~60 of ~78) asserted/medium if used.
[N-13] First Anglo-Burmese War: 10,000+ troops by sea into Rangoon 11 May 1824; Bay of Bengal transport convoys 1824–26; Burma no blue-water navy — commerce menace negligible. counted/high.
[N-14] Opium War force: 37 warships + steamers; Pearl River blockaded from Jun 1840; yet regular trade continued at Canton largely via neutral American carriers; 1842 Yangtze blockade forced peace. counted/high. Risk asymmetric: British/Qing flags menaced, neutrals profited.
[N-15] Treaty of Nanking (29 Aug 1842): five treaty ports (Canton, Amoy, Foochow, Ningpo, Shanghai) from 1843 — a lane-structure change, not a war risk. counted/high.
[N-16] Mexican–American War: US blockade declared 14 May 1846 (Veracruz/Alvarado/Tampico/Matamoros); ~10,000 troops landed Veracruz Mar 1847; Pacific Squadron blockaded Mazatlán late 1846; Mexico's navy negligible, no privateers — uplift on MEXICAN flag only. counted/high.
[N-17] Bombardment of Algiers 27 Aug 1816 (Exmouth): 1,083 slaves freed immediately, 3,000+ total; Dey bound to end Christian enslavement; corsairing declined sharply but did NOT fully end (rebuilt; 1824 re-enforcement). counted/high.
[N-18] Residual Algerine risk ends permanently with the French blockade 1827–30 and conquest July 1830; Tunis/Tripoli had renounced 1816–19. Model Med corsair uplift decaying 1816, ZERO after mid-1830. counted/high.
[N-19] Portuguese Liberal Wars: Napier destroyed the Miguelite fleet off Cape St Vincent 5 Jul 1833 ("most of the fleet captured" — source tallies inconsistent); commerce menace localized (Portuguese coast, Azores; Terceira blockade 1829–31 asserted). counted/high.
[N-20] First Carlist War: RN controlled the Cantabrian coast from Apr 1834; coastal-only, no ocean-lane dimension — recommend NO wars.json entry (or note-only ~1.0–1.1). proxied/medium.
[N-21] Texas Navy (1836–37, 1839–43): harassed Mexican Gulf ports; Campeche 16 May 1843 draw lifted the Yucatán blockade; menace confined to Mexican coastal traffic. counted/high.
[N-22] Pastry War: France blockaded ALL Mexican Gulf ports 1838 (formal from April), took Veracruz/San Juan de Ulúa Nov–Dec 1838, withdrew 9 Mar 1839 — ~a year of closed Mexican Gulf ports; danger = port closure, not sea loss. counted/high.
[N-23] French blockade of the Plata 28 Mar 1838–29 Oct 1840 (11+ warships): Buenos Aires closed (80%-tariff-revenue figure = proxied/medium), Montevideo captured diverted trade. counted-dates/high.
[N-24] Anglo-French blockade of Buenos Aires declared 18 Sep 1845; 90-ship merchant convoy forced the Paraná past Obligado 20 NOV 1845 (fetched page's "1846" garbled); Britain settled Mar 1849 (blockade lifted from mid-1847), France 31 Aug 1850; Montevideo + up-river trade BOOSTED. counted/high.
[N-25] South China Sea piracy 1840s: fleets of 50–70 armed junks; RN 1849 campaign destroyed Chui A-poo's (23 junks) and Shap-ng-tsai's (~58–64 junks, ~1,700 men) fleets; victims overwhelmingly Chinese coastal traffic + opium clippers. Uplift on China-coast/junk lanes 1843–49, easing after Oct 1849. counted/high.
[N-26] Post-1815 RN laid up to ~one-sixth of 1813 peak tonnage in commission, dispersed to stations. proxied/medium.
[N-27] Convoy lapsed with the 1815 peace, never revived as routine (formal abolition acquiesced ~1872); post-1815 convoys episodic and local (Aegean 1827, Paraná 1845). Ships sail INDEPENDENTLY after 1815. proxied/medium.
[N-28] US distant stations: Mediterranean 1815, Pacific c.1821, West Indies 1822, Brazil 1826, East India 1835, Home 1841, Africa 1842 (Webster–Ashburton) — ambient naval traffic, not war risk; scripted-spawn material. counted/high.
[N-29] Peacetime posture per navy: France = coercive-blockade expeditionary (Algiers/Mexico/Plata); Spain = broken, nil after 1829; Netherlands = East Indies station; Russia = episodic Med (Navarino). asserted/medium (each supporting event sourced above).
