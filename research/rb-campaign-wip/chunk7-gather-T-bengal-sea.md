[T-26] Manila liberalization stepwise: 1789 / 1818 / 1830 / universal 1834 (RPC privileges lapse); foreign trade de facto dominant already in the 1820s. counted/high.

## PATTERNS
Bengal/Calcutta: UP every decade (1840s ≈ 2–2.5× the 1810s); statutory breaks 1813/1833 as slope changes; opium share climbing; coal/jute only trace pre-1850; 1839–42 = two-year sawtooth on the China legs, not a stop.
Opium carriage multipliers (on the verified system): 1810s ≈0.5× of the 1830 level, 1820s ≈0.8×, 1830s ≈1.8×, 1840s ≈2.8× (~50k chests 1848–49); route shifts to Calcutta→Singapore→Canton after 1819/1824; Bombay/Malwa ≈ half the total from the late 1820s (weight lanes near-equal post-1830); Hong Kong added as terminus after 1842.
Singapore entrepôt (NEW): counted backbone $0.4M→$25.2M with the real mid-1830s dip; THREE interleaved seasonal flows (China junks Jan–Mar/May; Bugis prahus Sept–Oct; year-round square-rig to Calcutta/Canton/London); native craft ~55% of regional tonnage sliding to ~43%; free-port = counted-with-declared-boundary; explosive 1819–25, plateau-with-dip 1826–37 (Dutch duties + NHM diversion), renewed growth 1838–50.
NHM/Java (NEW, replaces the VOC-successor pattern): weak 1820s → 1830 regime FLIP (state consignment, Dutch bottoms only, ~2× freight rates) → ~4–6× jump in the 1830s → sustained 1840s; fleet 9→49→92 ships 1823–35; thick Batavia↔Amsterdam, thin Batavia↔Singapore; junks now terminate at Singapore.
Manila (NEW): two-phase ramp — galleon taper 1815→1823 (Mexico's exclusion the killer), foreign square-rig growth from the partial openings to unrestricted 1834; sugar/abaca/tobacco out; single-node through 1850; level ASSERTED (direction reconstructed) — Legarda's tables the upgrade path.
Margins: Bugis reorients onto Singapore from 1819, holds ~half of regional carriage through the 1840s; Sulu raiding live through the 1830s, EASING 1843–49 (Dido/Brooke); Siam junk-carried all window (Burney changes the frame, not volumes — no inflection before 1855); Cochin-China small junk flow; Burma = small Moulmein teak node ~1828–41 then flat-declining, rice post-1852; the ~80k-ton junk economy at large → silences register (known, unquantified per-lane, NOT shrinking).
