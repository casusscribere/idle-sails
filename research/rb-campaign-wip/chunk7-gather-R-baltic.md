## PATTERNS (per-system guidance)
baltic-grain-west: 1810s hold w/ lane weight → Amsterdam/Hamburg (Corn Law shuts London); 1820s mild recovery; 1830s split (deep UK trough 1832–37 — floor, not zero: bonding — then the 1838–42 surge); 1840s strong, POST-1846 STEP-CHANGE 1.5–2× with UK lanes taking the growth; Danzig stays Europe's #2 wheat port behind Odessa.
baltic-timber-naval-west: halve LONDON-lane shares 1815–40 (80% of British softwood is BNA) while continental lanes hold; still ~600k loads to Britain in 1840 — thinner, never absent; stepwise recovery after 1842/1846; nudge Memel up.
swedish-iron-west: step down ~15%/decade 1815–45, flat after; the displaced volume went to the US (out of basin — silences cross-ref); British niche = oregrounds.
baltic-general/return/intra: scale on the Sound index 0.55 (1815) → 0.70 → 0.95 (1830) → 0.65 (1835) → 1.0 (1840) → 1.25 (1850); flag drift (British largest; Prussian #2 from mid-1820s; Norwegian/Danish/Dutch/small-German rising); return-east staples = coal/salt/colonial goods; intra weights the small German flags up.
petersburg-west: strong growth all decades; mostly foreign-carried; hemp/flax/tallow (tallow peak value to mid-1840s); London share ≥0.55; ~1,200–1,500 arrivals/yr (1820s) → 2,000+ (1840s), 1830s dip.
norway-timber-fish-west: +~30%/decade; tilt toward London (preference advantage); biggest jump post-1849 (epilogue).
english-coal-foreign: growing; ADD Copenhagen + St Petersburg destination lanes at small shares by the 1830s–40s.
north-sea-shorthaul: sail holds; from 1824 GSN steam captures the packet layer — evidence note per decade rather than shrinkage before 1845; late 1840s shave 10–15% + boundary marker.
NEW: german-emigration-atlantic (Bremen→USA from ~1830, ~10k/yr by 1832, tobacco/cotton return; honest Le Havre note; belongs to the ATLANTIC fold). north-sea-steam-packets = boundary/scripted layer, NOT a flow system.
