- [X-08] mortality: early voyages ~5–10%; **Second Fleet (arr. 3 June 1790) ~26% died on voyage, ~40% within 8 months** (contractor per-head incentives; counted); after **Redfern's 1814 report** naval surgeon-superintendents on all ships from **1815**, voyage mortality fell to ~2–3% (counted/proxied HIGH for the arc). Natural pre-1815/post-1815 mortality shift for the lane.

**Free commercial flows:**
- [X-09] whaling/sealing: Sydney a major S-Pacific base; whaling shifted west after 1797; first Sydney-owned whaler *King George* June 1805; sealing/whaling out-earned farming until the 1830s; **exports £3.3M 1831–1845** (counted/proxied MED-HIGH). Fits PLAN-4 E3 grounds-node (Tasman/SE-Australian bay whaling + South Sea grounds).
- [X-10] Bass Strait sealing >127,000 skins exported by 1809 (counted/MED, single operator/Underwood).
- [X-11] **wool boom**: 175,000 lb (1821) → 400,000 (1823) → 1,100,000 (1830) → 3,693,000 (1836); wool surpasses whale oil as principal export by the 1830s, dominant 1840s (Macarthur merino; counted/HIGH). Sydney→Britain lane REVERSES character: convicts/emigrants in, wool out.
- [X-12] China/Canton tea: First Fleet transports sailed on to Canton; colonial triangular trade manufactures→SW Pacific islands (sandalwood)→Canton (tea)→Sydney/Britain (proxied/reconstructed MED; per-year counts not gathered — magnitude gap).
- [X-13] free/assisted emigration inbound: bounty scheme 1835–1841; free-passage late 1830s; mass emigration post-1840 (esp. Irish 1840s) (counted/proxied MED-HIGH; per-year volumes not gathered).
