# Chunk 10 gather E4 — Hudson's Bay Company / York Factory (raw, pre-refutation)

**VERDICT: VERIFIED-WITH-RESTATEMENTS.** Counted-class claim holds; season-gated single-lane sim shape sound. Two restatements: (a) era-start + node identity; (b) the "1668–1815 continuous" span has a real ~20-yr French-occupation interruption.

[E4-01] **HBCA "ships' histories" register EXISTS as described** (counted/HIGH). Hudson's Bay Company Archives, Archives of Manitoba (real institution): searchable table, alphabetical, ~270+ vessels, 1668–1974; each entry = name · type/rig · service dates · service areas; most link to PDF history sheets; a parallel Section C finding aid covers 72 ships w/ underlying records (logs 1751–1920). **Adversarial caveat:** HBCA itself calls these *derived compilations* ("a good starting point… not comprehensive," advises checking primaries) → **counted at the register level, but a curated secondary layer over primary records** — note the caveat, don't claim raw-archival precision.

[E4-02] **1668 founding voyage VERIFIED but forces an era restatement** (HIGH). Two ships left Deptford 5 June 1668: *Nonsuch* (Gillam, w/ Groseilliers) + *Eaglet* (Stannard, w/ Radisson). **Eaglet turned back off Ireland; only *Nonsuch* reached the bay**, wintered 1668–69, built **Charles Fort at the Rupert River mouth (JAMES Bay)**, returned to England 9 Oct 1669. **HBC chartered 2 May 1670.** → 1668 = the pre-charter proving voyage to **Charles Fort/Rupert River, NOT York Factory** (which didn't exist). A node labelled "York Factory" **cannot honestly start at 1668/1670**.
  - **Option A (preferred):** node = HBC Hudson Bay trade, **era 1670–1815** (charter), with **York Factory as the dominant era-name from 1684** and Charles Fort/Rupert House the earlier era-name (the existing eraNames idiom). Gives the continuous English sub-Arctic presence.
  - **Option B:** strict node = York Factory, **era 1684–1815**.

[E4-03] **York Factory = the correct single node, w/ founding-date + interruption correction** (HIGH). York Factory (Hayes/Nelson River) was the HBC's central Bay depot/HQ — right over Albany/Churchill/Moose/Rupert House. **Founded 1684** (Fort Nelson 1684). **French occupation is real & material:** d'Iberville captured it **1694** (renamed Fort Bourbon); held by France ~1697–1713, **ceded back to Britain at Utrecht 1713** (English resumed 1714). → **"1668–1815 continuous" is FALSE for York specifically** — a ~1694–1714 British gap. Model via `active {from,to}` lifecycle window (like Kaffa/Smeerenburg) OR reroute to Albany during the occupation (option A).

[E4-04] **~1–3 supply ships/yr VERIFIED** (counted/reconstructed HIGH). The whole company ran a small tightly-scheduled annual fleet: a period source gives "by 1749 the annual trade consisted only of… three or four shiploads" for the ENTIRE company serving 4–5 forts, ~120 employees → **~1–2 ships/yr to York specifically, ~2–4 company-wide.** The claimed 1–3/yr band is realistic — genuinely low-volume (Dejima pattern apt). Gentle 18th-c rise optional, not required. Class the vessel count **counted** (HBCA), the York-specific split **reconstructed**.

[E4-05] **Season-gating VERIFIED, strongly** (HIGH). Ships left the Thames ~31 May, via Orkney, round Cape Farewell into Hudson Strait; **safe window ~15 July–1 Oct**; company ships "appear only in August, September and October" in the bay; returns to London late Sept/early Oct before freeze-up. Fully validates the season-gated single-lane model — same shape as the Arkhangelsk Arctic corridor (no winter departures). Source: Manitoba Historical Society, "Navigation of Hudson Bay and Straits."

[E4-06] **Named ships VERIFIED** (counted/HIGH). *Nimble* — brigantine, 1792–93, Europe + Hudson/James Bay ✓. *Beaver* — sloop→brig, 1788–93 ✓ (distinct from the famous 1835 PNW steamship *Beaver* — don't conflate). Representative small brigs/sloops. *Nonsuch* (a hired ketch) + *Eaglet* 1668 likewise attested.

**Corrections for the refuter to carry:** (1) era-start not 1668 for a York node — use 1670+York-as-≥1684-eraName [A] or 1684 strict [B]; (2) "continuous 1668–1815" overstated — French occupation ~1694–1714; (3) magnitude ~1–3/yr right but it's ~3–4 company-wide / ~1–2 to York; (4) register is HBCA's curated compilation over primaries — flag, don't overclaim.
